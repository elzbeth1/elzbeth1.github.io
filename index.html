// FINANCE · RISK MODELING SYSTEM

> CREDIT_RISK_MODEL

> PROBLEM

Financial institutions face a critical challenge: identifying high-risk borrowers before a loan turns into a financial loss.

This project was designed to predict whether a loan applicant is likely to default by building a machine learning-driven credit risk classification system.

> DATA ANALYSIS & PREPROCESSING

Performed extensive exploratory data analysis to understand borrower behaviour, repayment history, income distribution, and financial risk patterns.

The preprocessing pipeline included:

  • Missing value imputation
  • Outlier handling
  • Categorical encoding
  • Feature engineering

> RISK MODELING APPROACH

Applied Weight of Evidence (WOE) transformation and Information Value (IV) analysis to improve feature interpretability and identify highly predictive variables.

Trained and compared Logistic Regression and XGBoost models to evaluate performance across different borrower risk profiles.

> MODEL EVALUATION

Instead of relying only on accuracy, the evaluation strategy focused on metrics commonly used in financial risk modeling.

  • Recall
  • ROC-AUC
  • KS Statistic
  • Gini Coefficient

to better measure the model’s ability to separate risky borrowers from reliable applicants while minimizing financial exposure caused by false negatives.

> MODEL SELECTION

Although XGBoost achieved slightly higher ROC-AUC and KS scores, Logistic Regression was selected as the final model due to its higher recall and interpretability.

In credit risk modeling, minimizing false negatives is critical because failing to identify high-risk borrowers can lead to significant financial loss.

Additionally, Logistic Regression provides:

  • transparent decision-making
  • easier regulatory interpretation
  • stronger alignment with industry practices

> RESULTS

0.98 ROC-AUC
94% RECALL
86% KS SCORE
0.97 GINI

> BUSINESS INSIGHTS

High loan-to-income ratio and credit utilization were strong indicators of default risk, suggesting financial stress among borrowers.

Past delinquency behaviour significantly increased the probability of default, while home ownership was associated with greater financial stability.

These findings aligned closely with real-world credit risk modeling principles and validated the model’s predictive behavior.

> DEPLOYMENT

The trained model was exported and deployed as an interactive Streamlit application, allowing real-time credit risk prediction through a production-style interface.

> TECH STACK

Python Scikit-learn XGBoost Logistic Regression Pandas NumPy Streamlit WOE / IV
